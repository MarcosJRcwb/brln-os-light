# Autofee Tag Glossary (EN)

This page explains, in practical terms, the tags shown in `Autofee Results`.

PT-BR version: `AUTOFEE_TAG_GLOSSARIO_PT_BR.md`

Notes:
- Tags are generated by backend decision logic and may evolve over time.
- Some tags are fixed literals (for example `stagnation`).
- Some are dynamic patterns (for example `stagnation-rN`, `seed:vol-15%`, `inb-120`).

## Core channel labels
- `sink`: channel mostly used to drain local balance (outbound-heavy).
- `source`: channel mostly used as liquidity source for the node.
- `router`: balanced routing profile (not strongly sink/source).
- `unknown`: not enough signal to classify confidently.
- `trend-up`: current context points fee upward.
- `trend-down`: current context points fee downward.
- `trend-flat`: no strong directional trend.

## Movement and execution controls
- `stepcap`: target was limited by per-round step cap.
- `stepcap-lock`: target wanted a move, but step cap kept `same-ppm`.
- `floor-lock`: final fee is pinned at floor.
- `floor-relax-stall`: floor was relaxed under stall logic to unlock convergence.
- `hold-small`: tiny change skipped to avoid fee churn.
- `same-ppm`: run ended with no fee change.
- `cooldown`: change blocked by cooldown window.
- `cooldown-profit`: extra cooldown to protect profitability.
- `cooldown-skip`: cooldown explicitly bypassed by rule.
- `rebal-recent`: recent rebalance detected (liquidity still settling).
- `rebal-attempt`: recent rebalance attempt detected.
- `rebal-recent-noup`: upward move blocked due to recent rebalance.

## Profit and lock protections
- `neg-margin`: estimated margin is negative.
- `negm+X%`: negative margin amplified by risk buffer (`X%`).
- `no-down-low`: downward move blocked due to weak/low signal.
- `no-down-neg-margin`: downward move blocked to avoid worsening negative economics.
- `global-neg-lock`: global negative-margin lock engaged.
- `lock-skip-no-chan-rebal`: lock skipped because channel has no relevant rebalance context.
- `lock-skip-sink-profit`: lock skipped to preserve sink profitability strategy.
- `profit-protect-lock`: explicit profit protection lock.
- `profit-protect-relax`: profit lock partially relaxed (controlled downward move).

## Market anchors and floor behavior
- `sink-floor`: sink-specific minimum floor applied.
- `outrate-floor`: floor anchored to observed outbound market rate.
- `revfloor`: floor anchored to revenue/history.
- `peg`: floor pegged to market/reference rate.
- `peg-grace`: peg grace period active.
- `peg-demand`: peg strengthened by demand signal.
- `peg-paused-stagnation`: peg pressure paused because stagnation logic has priority.
- `low-out-slow-up`: low outbound activity, so upward repricing is slowed.
- `low-out-noflow-cap`: no-flow condition caps upward movement.
- `no-signal-noup`: no reliable signal, so no blind fee increase.
- `no-signal-floor-relax`: floor relaxed due to missing quality signal.
- `rebal-floor-low-volume`: rebalance floor applied with low-volume warning.
- `floor-up-blocked-low-signal`: floor-driven increase blocked due to weak signal.

## Stagnation controls
- `stagnation`: stagnation mode active.
- `stagnation-pressure`: stronger stagnation pressure active.
- `normalize-out`: stagnation trying to normalize toward outbound signal.
- `normalize-rebal`: stagnation trying to normalize toward rebalance cost.
- `stagnation-floor`: stagnation-specific floor active.
- `stagnation-floor-relax`: stagnation floor relaxed to unblock movement.
- `stagnation-neg-override`: negative-margin override while in stagnation logic.
- `stagnation-rN`: number of consecutive stagnation rounds.
- `stagnation-cap-<ppm>`: temporary stagnation cap in ppm.

## Discovery, surge, and adaptive modes
- `discovery`: discovery mode active (searching better fee equilibrium).
- `discovery-hard`: aggressive discovery variant.
- `explorer`: exploratory behavior active for repricing.
- `surge+...`: temporary increase due to strong recent demand/flow.
- `surge-hold...`: hold phase after surge trigger.
- `surge-timeout-release`: surge hold released after timeout.
- `surge-confirmed-rounds`: surge confirmed across multiple rounds.
- `circuit-breaker`: safety breaker active.
- `extreme-drain`: aggressive drained-liquidity mode active.
- `extreme-drain-unlock`: temporary unlock while in extreme drain context.
- `extreme-drain-turbo`: turbo variant of extreme drain.
- `super-source`: channel treated as super source.
- `super-source-like`: channel behaves like super source.
- `top-rev`: top-revenue channel signal.

## HTLC signal tags
- `htlc-policy-hot`: policy failure pressure is high.
- `htlc-liquidity-hot`: liquidity failure pressure is high.
- `htlc-forward-hot`: forward failure pressure is high.
- `htlc-sample-low`: HTLC sample size too low for strong confidence.
- `htlc-neutral-lock`: neutral lock used under mixed/uncertain HTLC signal.
- `htlc-liq+X%`: incremental adjustment from HTLC liquidity pressure (`X%`).
- `htlc-policy+X%`: incremental adjustment from HTLC policy pressure (`X%`).
- `htlc-liq-nodown`: blocks downward move under hot liquidity signal.
- `htlc-policy-nodown`: blocks downward move under hot policy signal.
- `htlc-neutral-nodown`: conservative no-down rule under neutral HTLC state.
- `htlc-step-boost`: step boost caused by HTLC signal.

## New inbound and inbound discount
- `new-inbound`: recently opened inbound channel detected.
- `bootstrap`: bootstrap phase for new channel pricing.
- `inb-<n>`: inbound discount applied (`n` ppm).

## Seed and fallback provenance
- `seed:amboss*`: seed sourced from Amboss (or Amboss fallback status).
- `seed:med`: seed calibrated to medium profile.
- `seed:vol-<n>%`: seed adjusted by volatility factor (`n%`).
- `seed:ratio<factor>`: seed adjusted by ratio multiplier.
- `seed:outrate`: seed derived from outbound rate history.
- `seed:mem`: seed taken from local memory/history.
- `seed:default`: default seed used.
- `seed:guard`: seed guardrail applied.
- `seed:p95cap`: seed capped by p95 statistical cap.
- `seed:absmax`: seed capped by absolute maximum.
- `out-fallback-21d`: outbound fallback data from 21-day window.
- `rebal-fallback-21d`: rebalance fallback data from 21-day window.

## Forecast codes (line suffix)
- `stable` / `stable-uncertain`: no clear direction; wait for stronger signal.
- `bias_up` / `upward bias`: likely upward move in upcoming rounds.
- `bias_down` / `downward bias`: likely downward move in upcoming rounds.
- `hold_or_up`: maintain or raise to protect economics/liquidity.
- `reduce`: expected to reduce when guardrails allow.
- `idle_reduce`: expected reduction due to idle excess liquidity.
- `discovery_fast`: accelerated reduction under discovery mode.
